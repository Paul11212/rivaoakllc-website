Independent Living Housing Program (ILHP)

The ILHP is designed for adults who can manage their own Activities of Daily Living (ADLs) but need a safe, stable, and affordable place to live. We operate gender-specific, fully furnished homes in community settings — ideal for veterans, agency-referred clients, and adults on fixed incomes.

How It Works – Residents & Families

  1. Inquiry & Eligibility – Contact us or speak with your case manager to confirm basic eligibility and availability.
  2. Application & Screening – We review background, income source, and housing needs to ensure a safe, appropriate placement.
  3. Placement & Move-In – Once approved, we assign a bed, review house rules, and coordinate a smooth move-in.

Our expectation is that residents are capable of managing their own ADLs and living respectfully in a shared home environment.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this assisted living or a medical facility?

No. Riva Oak Independent Living provides independent living housing only. We do not provide medical care, nursing services, or personal care assistance.

Who is eligible to live in your homes?

Adults who can manage their Activities of Daily Living (ADLs) and live safely in a shared housing environment may be eligible. Some homes may have additional criteria based on referrals or funding source.

Do you accept veterans?

Yes. We welcome veterans and frequently work with VA case managers and veteran-serving organizations to coordinate placements.

Are your homes co-ed?

No. Our homes are gender-specific to promote comfort, privacy, and safety for residents.

What is included in the monthly rate?

Monthly rates typically include housing, furnishings, and utilities for the home. Specific details vary by property and will be confirmed during the intake process.

How long can someone stay?

Stays may be short-term or long-term depending on the resident’s needs, funding, and compliance with house rules and payment terms.
